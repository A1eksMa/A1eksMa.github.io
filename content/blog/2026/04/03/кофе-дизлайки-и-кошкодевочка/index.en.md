I have no idea what's keeping me going these past two weeks. Apparently, nothing but coffee.

I've been pouring it into myself by the liter — and it's the only thing that helps even a little. I work mostly at night, because the project has finally started moving. On top of that, I'm pulling double duty — one of our guys is on vacation and I have to cover for him somehow. I'm constantly hungry and greens aren't helping: I've dropped about three kilos in two weeks, and I've come to appreciate why Sakamoto Taro from Sakamoto Days ended up as my avatar. I've always admired his superpower of shedding weight at lightning speed. The cortisol spikes on top of all this aren't exactly doing wonders for my sleep.

---

March was rough. We lost part of the infrastructure, had to cut down, pull in reserves, and it hit the project hard. At some point I started having thoughts that maybe it was time to start winding things down.

Because to develop something even halfway serious, I need a bigger reach, more users, more hardware. As H. Thompson wrote:

> *Faster, faster, until the thrill of speed overcomes the fear of death.*

— and I think he meant exactly this, even if he was talking about something else entirely.

I also tried to find someone to handle the visuals on a bare minimum level: a logo, a mascot, that sort of thing. But when I saw the price, I thought: for that kind of money, I could draw not just a mascot — I could paint the Mona Lisa, and you wouldn't be able to tell the difference.

Understanding that nothing particularly new on the technical side would come out during this rough patch anyway, I decided to throw everything into PR. Starting last week, I went to Habr and published a few pieces there.

---

Habr is a specific platform. You probably only realize that once you become an author and start getting feedback: dislikes, comments, read-through graphs, retention funnels, conversion rates. Numbers.

Short version — I got buried in dislikes, the drop-off was heavy, and conversion was very low. Habr did not appreciate me. Not at all.

Enough impressions accumulated to warrant a dedicated special issue on the subject — while they're still fresh. That's exactly what we'll do.

The second big article, about AI agents, I ultimately didn't bring to Habr at all — only the April Fools' teaser went there. For the rest, I realized, I should probably head somewhere like Pikabu.

---

And then, just when things were looking genuinely bleak, people started showing up from Habr. And it turned out to be exactly the audience I had written "Notes of a Surviving Lemming" for.

People with a tech background, with a crystal-clear understanding of the situation, the possibilities, and what they want and why they're here. People who don't need anything explained, persuaded, or sold to them — they already know the inside story, they understand everything I'm trying to do here. With enough motivation and a little free time, any one of them could spin up and configure similar infrastructure on their own.

But — most of them have simply had it, zaeblo, to use the Russian term. Because every field has its own specifics: if you're not working in a narrow area full-time, you'll burn through a huge amount of time just getting up to speed on the current trends and figuring out "how things are done these days."

None of them had any trouble spinning up a server. Nobody needed an explanation of which button to press. People from Habr — you are remarkable.

---

The upshot of all of this: the show goes on. I have enough hardware to bring up a distributed cluster, and enough chrome in me to actually do it (I know that cyberpsychosis is coming for me sooner or later).

And then, right at the moment I was bringing new nodes online, someone sent me a sketch of the catgirl. Right at that exact moment. The person who drew it said they just felt the vibe and decided to go for it — and I had already stopped waiting, had practically forgotten about it. It was like finding a banknote in an old jacket you haven't worn since last year. It made my day.

Sometimes I get these inexplicable waves of love for all of humanity.

Take care, and have a good weekend.
