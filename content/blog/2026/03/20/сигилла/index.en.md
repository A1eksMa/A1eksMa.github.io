In the very [first post], I casually mentioned the idea of a mascot — a cat-girl sorceress with purple hair, a druid's hood, and a staff bearing **Sigil Gate** imagery. The concept came immediately, naturally — as soon as the visual direction of the project became clear.

I draw a little. Or rather — I used to. Now that programming has taken up most of my time, I pick up a pen once every couple of months — usually during the busiest stretches, to shift gears and breathe. Not to rest — just to shift. Drawing in those moments works like a kind of meditation: hands are busy, mind gets a break.

If I wanted to, I could probably sketch something out. But there's a catch: I work in traditional media — pencil, pen, paper (plus markers when the mood strikes). Converting a paper sketch into proper digital art is a whole separate story, one that requires time I don't have right now. So I took a different route: I reached out to some artists I know. Not sure if anyone will take it on — but I'm even willing to commission someone, just to get at least a rough sketch of the character.

This post is essentially a creative brief. I'll try to put into words what I have in my head.

---

## Colors

I [wrote before] that the dark theme of the site leans into deep purple tones — this matched the mood in which the project began. It was a hard winter. Early in the year, in the middle of winter, I was probably at my lowest point: isolated, emotionally drained, and the world felt dark and heavy. I think that's why I chose that theme.

Dark purple is the color of that period. And it's the color I want for the mascot.

The project's color palette, as I see it: **dark purple and blue** as the base, **red-pink and sky blue** as accents, everything on a dark background. In the mascot, this should read as:

- **Hair** — purple, with blue streaks.
- **Eyes** — purple, glowing — that effect where only two flickering lights are visible from under the hood, face hidden.
- **Clothing** — in the spirit of a druidic hooded cloak.

## The Character

In the post ["Secret Gates"], I talked about where the project's name came from — and it touched on druids, magical symbols, the city of Doors. All of that should be readable in the character.

This character, styled as chibi (a slightly cartoonish format with a large head and small body), is something I'd like to use for the site's visual identity: as an avatar, a virtual assistant accompanying user guides, stickers in the Telegram channel.

The character's name is **Sigilla** (the project name with Latin roots + *la*, a feminine suffix in Italian) — it seems to fit her perfectly.

She should be a cute and charming character, expressive and full of emotion, with cat ears, in a dark magic setting.

I think she should have a lizard's scaly tail — one that occasionally detaches, causing a degree of awkwardness (especially when it happens at the worst possible moment). This is meant to symbolize the project's architectural principle of continuous regeneration and the painless loss of parts of the network infrastructure.

In terms of props and clothing:

- A druid's hood, from under which only the eyes are visible. A nod to the image of the typical hacker in popular culture.

- A magic staff in hand — bearing **Sigil Gate**'s signature iconography (I have a few ideas about using it as a logo, but that's a separate topic).

- A keyring — on the belt or in the other hand, as a symbol of restricted access.

- A circle with runes in the background, possibly blurred and indistinct: a sigil is not a single rune but a specific combination of runes. I like how Cromwell's seal looks in Hellsing — I think something similar is needed here, but larger.

There's probably more to add, but my imagination has its limits.

## Logo

This is easier to draw — but I'll try to describe it in words. Maybe some AI can generate something close from my description.

The network architecture is built on a core-and-periphery principle, with a central management node integrated into the core.

The management node (root) — a central circle, with connections running to core nodes: three smaller circles, interconnected. All of this sits inside a circle representing the core. Beyond that circle, connections run from each core node to even smaller circles — shell nodes, three per core node. The connections could be depicted as small lightning bolts or electrical discharges.

All of this could also be rendered spatially — as a small solar system, replacing circles with spheres and arranging them in a more chaotic layout.
