Last autumn, my old VPN server went down. For several years it had done its job faithfully: a cloud server running xray, nothing extra. A simple solution that worked right up until it didn't.

It was a good lesson: simple solutions don't work anymore, and a single server is also a single point of failure that takes everything down with it. Like a domino effect, it knocked out remote infrastructure access, the IDE, online courses, and AI services all in a chain. It became clear: I needed to build something systemic. Not a stopgap — infrastructure. Not a server — a network.

The project began with that decision. And the first thought that followed: I need a name. Because what you name your boat is how it will sail.

---

The first candidate was **Digital Fortress**. It felt strong — a digital stronghold, a protected perimeter, all of that. I was already mentally registering domains — and then I discovered I'd been beaten to it. On multiple fronts: the names were taken on GitHub, and in literature I'd been scooped by none other than Dan Brown. A novel from 1998, film adaptations, millions of copies sold.

Fair enough. As you may know, I consciously avoid overly popular patterns. So I started looking for alternatives.

---

We ran a small brainstorm with friends. And at some point in the discussion, a variant emerged that clearly resonated with the meanings I wanted to embed in the project — **Sigil Gate**.

**Sigil** — magical runes. In the medieval occult tradition, this was the word for the personal seal of a spirit or angel: a unique mark carrying power and intention. A sigil is typically not a single symbol but a unique combination — a key that grants access.

**Gate** — a gateway. A passage. A place of transition from one state to another.

Together, it reads on several levels at once. Cryptographic: signatures, keys, multi-layered authentication — all of it is literally written into the name. Architectural: the network as a system of gates through which only those who know the right sign may pass. And metaphysical — but more on that in a moment.

---

I got into D&D not that long ago — and discovered that within that game's universe, there exists a place called **Sigil**. The City of Doors. A metropolis suspended at the top of the Infinite Spire, threaded through with thousands of portals to every world and plane of existence. Want to reach any point in the multiverse — go to Sigil, find the right door. The key is knowing how to open it.

I couldn't have invented a better metaphor for what I'm building.

That coincidence finally convinced me the name was right. But there was one more reason — entirely practical.

After Russia blocked the main anime platforms, watching favourite series became possible only through a VPN. I'm currently finishing the second season of *Frieren: Beyond Journey's End* — and that, when you think about it, is also one of the driving forces behind this entire project. So **Sigil Gate** is also an unspoken nod to Tolkien's fantasy worlds, anime culture, and all those universes whose accessibility shouldn't depend on anyone else's decisions.
